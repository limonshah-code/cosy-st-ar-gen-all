### Understanding CADR Ratings

Clean Air Delivery Rate, or CADR, measures how fast an air purifier cleans a room. It tells you the volume of clean air delivered per minute. CADR ratings apply to three types of pollutants: smoke, pollen, and dust. Higher CADR numbers mean faster cleaning. For cold relief, a high CADR for dust and smoke particles is important. These represent tiny airborne irritants and pathogens. Look for a CADR that is at least two-thirds the square footage of your room.

### Room Size and Air Changes Per Hour (ACH)

First, measure the square footage of the room where the sick person will spend most of their time. This is often a bedroom or living room. Air purifiers specify the maximum room size they can effectively cover. For cold season, aim for an air purifier that offers at least 4-5 Air Changes Per Hour (ACH). This means the purifier cleans and filters all the air in the room 4 to 5 times every hour. More air changes mean faster removal of airborne viruses and irritants.

## Maintenance and Filter Replacement for Peak Performance

An air purifier is only as effective as its filters. Dirty filters cannot trap particles efficiently. This means your air purifier might not help with cold symptoms. Regular maintenance keeps your unit running at its best. It ensures you always get clean air. I learned that ignoring filter changes wastes money and reduces protection.

### Checking and Cleaning Pre-Filters

Most air purifiers have a pre-filter. This filter catches larger particles. Think of dust, lint, and pet hair. This layer protects the more delicate HEPA filter. Pre-filters are often washable or vacuumable. Check your pre-filter every 2-4 weeks. Clean it as recommended by the manufacturer. A dirty pre-filter reduces airflow. This makes the entire unit work harder. It also lessens its ability to clean the air. Regular cleaning extends the life of your other filters. This saves you money in the long run.

### Replacing HEPA and Activated Carbon Filters

HEPA and activated carbon filters have a limited lifespan. They get full of trapped particles. Once full, they cannot absorb new pollutants. Most manufacturers recommend replacing HEPA filters every 6-12 months. Activated carbon filters might need changing every 3-6 months. This depends on air quality and usage. You might need to change filters more often during cold and flu season. High levels of airborne particles will fill filters faster.

Signs it is time for a filter change include:
*   **Reduced airflow:** The fan might run, but you feel less air coming out.
*   **Increased noise:** The motor might strain.
*   **Unpleasant odors:** The activated carbon filter might be saturated.
*   **Indicator light:** Many modern purifiers have a filter change indicator.

Neglecting filter changes harms your health. The purifier cannot remove viruses and allergens. This means less protection during a cold. It also wastes energy. The unit tries to push air through clogged filters. Investing in new filters is part of owning an air purifier. It ensures you get clean air when you need it most.
