## Meet the White Swiss Shepherd: A Distinct Breed

When you see a dog that looks like a German Shepherd but is all white, you are most likely looking at a White Swiss Shepherd. This breed is formally known as the Berger Blanc Suisse. It gained official recognition as a separate breed by the Fédération Cynologique Internationale (FCI) in 2003. This recognition made it distinct from the German Shepherd Dog.

The White Swiss Shepherd carries a pure white coat. Its eyes are typically dark, and its nose and lips are black. This striking contrast adds to its beauty. The breed possesses a well-muscled body, with an athletic and graceful build. People often describe their expression as intelligent and friendly.

White Swiss Shepherds share many physical traits with their colored German Shepherd cousins. They have the same strong bones and similar body proportions. However, their temperament often differs slightly. Many find the White Swiss Shepherd to be softer and less intense than some German Shepherd lines. They are known for being very loyal to their families. They also show great affection, making them wonderful companions.

## Tracing the White German Shepherd Lineage

The history of white-coated dogs within the German Shepherd lineage is quite interesting. The original German Shepherd breed standard did not exclude white coats. Early German Shepherds sometimes produced white puppies. These white dogs were simply a color variation.

Horation von Grafrath, the grandfather of the first German Shepherd, Hektor Linksrhein (later Horand von Grafrath), had a white ancestor. This means the white coat gene was present from the very beginning of the breed. However, over time, the white coat became less favored. Some breeders believed the white coat was a sign of albinism or genetic weakness. This belief was incorrect. The white coat is caused by a recessive gene that only suppresses pigment in the coat. It does not affect eye or skin pigment.

The American Kennel Club (AKC) once disqualified white German Shepherds from conformation shows in 1968. This decision pushed breeders who loved the white dogs to establish separate lines. In Canada and the United States, some breeders continued to breed white German Shepherds. These dogs retained their German Shepherd registration, but their white color was often noted. The exclusion led to the development of the White Swiss Shepherd in Europe. These white dogs are part of the broader group of [dogs that look like a German Shepherd but are not German Shepherds](https://cosyhomecreation.com/what-dog-looks-like-a-german-shepherd-but-is-not-a-german-shepherd).

## Caring for Your White Shepherd Look-Alike

Caring for a dog that looks like a German Shepherd but is all white involves attention to their unique needs. Their white coat needs regular grooming to stay clean and healthy. These dogs typically have a double coat, consisting of a dense undercoat and a coarser outer coat. They shed, especially during seasonal changes.

Brushing should happen several times a week. This helps remove loose hair and prevents matting. Regular brushing also distributes natural oils, keeping their coat shiny. During heavy shedding periods, daily brushing is helpful. Bathing can be done as needed, but not too frequently, as it can strip natural oils. Use a shampoo designed for white coats to help keep their fur bright.

Health is another important aspect. White Swiss Shepherds and White German Shepherds are generally healthy breeds. However, like all large breeds, they can be prone to certain genetic conditions. These include hip and elbow dysplasia. Responsible breeders screen their dogs for these conditions. Regular veterinary check-ups are vital for early detection and preventative care. ## FAQ Section

### Are White German Shepherds prone to more health problems than colored ones?
No, white German Shepherds are not inherently prone to more health problems due to their white coat. The white color is a recessive gene affecting pigment, not a sign of albinism or genetic weakness. They share similar health predispositions, like hip and elbow dysplasia, as other German Shepherds.

### Is the White Swiss Shepherd a recognized breed?
Yes, the White Swiss Shepherd (Berger Blanc Suisse) is a fully recognized breed by the Fédération Cynologique Internationale (FCI). This global kennel organization officially recognized the breed in 2003. Many other international kennel clubs also recognize it as a distinct breed.

### Do White German Shepherds or White Swiss Shepherds have red eyes?
No, dogs that look like a German Shepherd but are all white, whether White Swiss Shepherds or White German Shepherds, do not have red eyes. Their eyes are typically dark brown or amber. Red eyes would indicate albinism, which is a different genetic condition not associated with their white coats.

### Are white-coated shepherds good with children and other pets?
Yes, white-coated shepherds, especially White Swiss Shepherds, are generally known for being good with children and other pets. They often have a gentle and patient temperament. Proper socialization from a young age is still essential to ensure they grow into well-adjusted and safe family members.

### How much exercise does a white-coated German Shepherd look-alike need?
These intelligent and active dogs require a good amount of exercise. Plan for at least 60-90 minutes of daily physical activity. This can include brisk walks, jogging, playing fetch, or engaging in dog sports. Mental stimulation through training and puzzle toys is equally important to keep them happy.
