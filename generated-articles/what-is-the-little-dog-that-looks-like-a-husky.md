### The Alaskan Klee Kai: A True Miniature Husky

The Alaskan Klee Kai stands out as the quintessential **little dog that looks like a husky**. This breed was specifically developed in the 1970s in Alaska to be a companion-sized version of the Siberian Husky. "Klee Kai" translates to "small dog" in an Inuit dialect, accurately reflecting their size. They possess all the characteristic traits you love about Huskies, just miniaturized.

Alaskan Klee Kais come in three sizes: toy, miniature, and standard, ranging from 10 to 17 inches tall and weighing between 6 to 22 pounds. They have the same striking facial masks, sharp ears, and thick double coats as their larger counterparts. Their eye colors can vary, often displaying the captivating blue, brown, or even bi-colored eyes famously associated with Huskies. Their intelligent and curious nature makes them engaging companions. However, they can be reserved with strangers and benefit from early socialization.

These dogs are known for being vocal, often emitting playful "woo-woos" rather than barks, much like a full-sized Husky. Despite their small size, Klee Kais are energetic and require regular exercise to stay happy and healthy. Their grooming needs are similar to Huskies; they shed seasonally and need regular brushing to maintain their coat. If you are looking for a miniature Husky that is a recognized breed, the Alaskan Klee Kai is your top choice.

### The Pomsky: A Popular Husky Hybrid

Another prominent answer to "What is the **little dog that looks like a husky**?" is the Pomsky. This is a designer dog breed, a cross between a Pomeranian and a Siberian Husky. The Pomsky's appearance can vary significantly, depending on which parent breed's genes are more dominant. This variety means each Pomsky is unique.

Pomskies typically range from 10 to 15 inches in height and weigh between 12 to 30 pounds. They often inherit the Husky's striking blue eyes and facial markings, combined with the Pomeranian's fluffy coat and smaller stature. Their ears can be erect like a Husky or slightly floppier like a Pomeranian. Many Pomskies truly capture the "miniature Husky" look, especially those with more Husky-like features. Their temperament is a blend of both parent breeds. They can be playful and energetic like Huskies, but also affectionate and cuddly like Pomeranians. Training and socialization are crucial for Pomskies, as they can sometimes inherit the stubbornness of Huskies or the "small dog syndrome" seen in some Pomeranians. Their double coat means they are moderate to heavy shredders and require regular grooming. The Pomsky offers a compelling option for those seeking a **little dog that looks like a husky** with a slightly more unpredictable but often charming personality.

### Miniature Siberian Huskies: A Breed Standard Variation

When people search for a **little dog that looks like a husky**, they might also be referring to Miniature Siberian Huskies. It is important to understand that the Miniature Siberian Husky is not a distinct breed recognized by major kennel clubs like the AKC. Instead, they are simply smaller examples of the standard Siberian Husky breed. Breeders sometimes selectively breed smaller Huskies together to produce smaller offspring.

These smaller Huskies still fall within the Siberian Husky breed standard, just at the lower end of the weight and height spectrum, or sometimes even slightly below it. They generally retain all the physical characteristics of a standard Husky, including the lean, athletic build, erect ears, striking eyes, and distinctive coat patterns. A standard Siberian Husky typically weighs 35-60 pounds and stands 20-23.5 inches tall. A "miniature" version might be 18-20 inches tall and weigh 25-35 pounds.

The temperament and exercise needs of Miniature Siberian Huskies are essentially the same as their larger counterparts. They are still highly energetic, intelligent, and vocal dogs that thrive on activity and companionship. They are known for their independent and sometimes mischievous nature. ### Exploring Other Husky-Like Small Dogs

While the Alaskan Klee Kai and Pomsky are the most direct answers to "What is the **little dog that looks like a husky**?", a few other breeds might sometimes be confused or offer a similar aesthetic. These dogs may share certain Husky features, such as erect ears, a thick coat, or specific color patterns, but often do not have the exact striking facial mask or eye color.

One such dog is the American Eskimo Dog, particularly the miniature or standard sizes. They have a thick white double coat and often have alert, intelligent expressions. While they lack the specific markings of a Husky, their Spitz-type appearance can sometimes evoke a similar feeling. Another consideration might be the Finnish Spitz, known for its foxy appearance, thick coat, and curled tail. They are typically reddish-gold but share the Northern dog characteristics. These breeds are generally friendly and good with families but have different energy levels and temperaments than a true Husky or Klee Kai.

It is also worth noting that sometimes, people might encounter mixed-breed dogs that happen to inherit Husky-like features from one of their parents, even if the other parent is a smaller breed. These accidental mixes can sometimes surprise owners with their Husky resemblance. However, their specific characteristics will depend entirely on their unique genetic makeup.
