### Why Your Tankless Water Heater Needs a Filter

Tankless water heaters heat water on demand. They do not store hot water like traditional tank models. This design makes them highly efficient. However, it also makes them vulnerable to impurities in your water supply. Sediment, hard water minerals, and chlorine quickly build up inside the small heat exchangers.

Scale buildup is a major concern. Hard water contains dissolved minerals like calcium and magnesium. When heated, these minerals form solid deposits. These deposits cling to the heating elements and pipes inside your tankless unit. This layer of scale acts as an insulator. It forces the heater to work harder to warm the water. Over time, this reduces efficiency and wastes energy. It also damages internal components.

Sediment also poses a threat. Dirt, rust, and other particles can enter your home's plumbing. These particles can clog small passages within the tankless heater. This restricts water flow. It can even cause the unit to fail completely. Chlorine in municipal water can also corrode internal parts over time. A water filter stops these problems. It keeps your tankless water heater clean inside. This ensures it operates smoothly for many years. It also saves you money on energy bills and potential repairs.
