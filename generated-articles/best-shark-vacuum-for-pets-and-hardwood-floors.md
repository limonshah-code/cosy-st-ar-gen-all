## Maintaining Your Shark Vacuum for Optimal Performance

Maintaining your Shark vacuum is crucial for its longevity and continued high performance. This is especially true when you have pets, as pet hair and dander can quickly clog filters and brushrolls. Regular maintenance ensures your vacuum always cleans efficiently. I always make sure to keep up with these simple steps to prolong my vacuum's life.

### Cleaning Filters Regularly

Filters are the unsung heroes of your vacuum. They trap dust, allergens, and pet dander. Over time, these filters become saturated, reducing suction power. Most Shark vacuums have washable foam and felt filters. You should rinse these filters with cold water every month, or more often if you have multiple pets. Allow them to air dry completely for at least 24 hours before putting them back in. Some models also have a post-motor HEPA filter. This filter usually needs cleaning less frequently, perhaps once a year, or a gentle tap to remove loose dust. A clean filter ensures maximum airflow and suction.
