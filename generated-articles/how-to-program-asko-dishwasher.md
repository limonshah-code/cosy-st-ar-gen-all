## Setting Up Basic Wash Programs on Your Asko

Asko dishwashers come with several basic wash programs. These programs are designed for different types of loads. Choosing the correct program helps clean your dishes effectively. It also saves water and energy. Understanding these options is key to knowing how to program Asko dishwasher cycles.

Common programs include "Normal," "Heavy," "Quick," and "Eco." The "Normal" program works well for daily loads. It cleans typical amounts of food soil. The "Heavy" program is for pots, pans, and heavily soiled items. This cycle uses more water and higher temperatures. I use it for tough messes.

The "Quick" program is for lightly soiled dishes. It finishes in a shorter time. The "Eco" program saves energy and water. It often runs longer but uses less electricity. You can select these programs by pressing the program button. The indicator light next to your choice will light up.

Some Asko models offer even more specific programs. These might include "Crystal" for delicate glassware or "Hygiene" for extra sanitization. My advice is to match the program to your dish load. For example, do not use the "Heavy" program for lightly soiled glasses. This saves resources and protects your dishes.

*   **Normal:** For everyday dishes with normal food residue.
*   **Heavy/Intensive:** For pots, pans, and very dirty dishes.
*   **Quick/Rapid:** For lightly soiled dishes that need a fast wash.
*   **Eco:** For energy and water efficiency, typically a longer cycle.
*   **Delicate/Crystal:** For fragile items like wine glasses.
*   **Rinse & Hold:** To rinse dishes before a full wash.

Selecting the right wash program is a simple process. Press the program selector button repeatedly. Watch the lights or display. Stop when the desired program is highlighted. Then, you can press the start button. This action begins the washing process.

## Troubleshooting Common Asko Dishwasher Programming Issues

Sometimes, your Asko dishwasher may not respond as expected. Programming issues can occur. Knowing how to troubleshoot these problems saves time and frustration. It helps ensure your Asko dishwasher runs smoothly.

One common issue is the dishwasher not starting. First, check if the door is fully closed. Asko dishwashers have safety interlocks. If the door is not latched, the cycle will not begin. Ensure no dishes block the latch. Also, check the power supply. Make sure the dishwasher is plugged in. Check the circuit breaker. If the circuit breaker tripped, reset it.

Another problem might be a program not completing. This can happen if water is not draining. A clogged drain hose or filter can cause this. You might hear the dishwasher attempting to pump water. If your dishwasher is not draining, there are specific steps to resolve it, which can be found in guides like [how to get the dishwasher to drain](https://cosyhomecreation.com/how-to-get-the-dishwasher-to-drain) or [how to drain dishwasher water manually](https://cosyhomecreation.com/how-to-drain-dishwasher-water-manually). Clear any blockages. Check the air gap if your system has one.

Error codes on the display indicate specific problems. These codes usually correspond to issues listed in your user manual. For example, an E1 or F1 code might point to a water supply problem. A leak might trigger a specific code, and if you are dealing with a leak, understanding [how to repair a leaking dishwasher](https://cosyhomecreation.com/how-to-repair-a-leaking-dishwasher) could be useful. Consult your manual immediately if you see an error code. It provides the meaning of the code and steps to fix it.

If the control panel is unresponsive, try a power reset. Turn off the dishwasher at the circuit breaker. Wait a few minutes, then turn it back on. This often clears minor electronic glitches. This simple step can fix many programming issues. My experience shows this often resolves the problem quickly.

If problems persist, contact Asko customer support. They can provide specific troubleshooting steps. They can also arrange for a service technician. Do not attempt complex repairs yourself. This can void your warranty or cause further damage. Professional help is best for serious issues.

## FAQ Section

### How do I reset my Asko dishwasher?

To reset most Asko dishwashers, you can usually press and hold the "Start" or "Cancel" button for a few seconds. Alternatively, you can power cycle the unit by turning off the circuit breaker for the dishwasher for about 5 minutes, then turning it back on. This often clears minor glitches and resets the control panel.

### Can I change a program mid-cycle on an Asko?

Yes, on most Asko dishwashers, you can change or stop a program mid-cycle. Press and hold the "Start/Stop" or "Cancel" button for a few seconds. This usually drains the water and resets the program. Then, you can select a new program and restart the cycle. Always refer to your specific model's manual for exact instructions.

### What is the best program for heavily soiled dishes on an Asko?

For heavily soiled dishes like pots and pans, the "Heavy" or "Intensive" program is usually the best choice on an Asko dishwasher. This program uses higher temperatures and more water, along with extended wash times, to remove stubborn food residue. Some models may also have a "Pots & Pans" cycle.

### How do I adjust the water hardness setting on my Asko dishwasher?

Adjusting water hardness on an Asko dishwasher often involves accessing a specific service menu. You typically press a combination of buttons (e.g., "Program" and "Start" simultaneously) at power-on to enter the setting mode. Then, use the program selector to set the desired hardness level. Consult your user manual for the precise button sequence for your model.

### Why won't my Asko dishwasher start when I program it?

If your Asko dishwasher won't start after programming, first check if the door is fully latched. The safety mechanism prevents operation if the door is not secure. Also, ensure the power cord is plugged in and the circuit breaker has not tripped. Some models have a delay start activated by mistake; check for a timer setting on the display.

### Where do I put rinse aid in my Asko dishwasher?

The rinse aid dispenser on an Asko dishwasher is typically located inside the door, next to the detergent dispenser. It usually has a circular cap with a small indicator window. Twist the cap to open it, fill the reservoir with liquid rinse aid until the indicator shows full, and then close the cap firmly.
