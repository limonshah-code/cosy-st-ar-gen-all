### Types of Filters in Dishwashers

Dishwashers typically use one of two main filter types. Some older models have a hard food disposer. This grinds up food particles. Newer, quieter models, like many Kenmore Elite dishwashers, use a manual filter system. This system requires regular cleaning.

A manual filter system has two parts. The first part is a coarse filter. This large screen catches bigger food pieces. The second part is a fine micro-filter. This cylinder traps tiny particles. Both work together to keep the wash water clean. They ensure your dishes get spotless.

### Why Your Dishwasher Has a Filter

The main purpose of a dishwasher filter is clear. It prevents food particles from circulating. Without a filter, these particles would redeposit on your dishes. This would leave them dirty or gritty. The filter also protects the dishwasher's pump. Small food bits can damage the pump over time.

A clean filter ensures optimal spray arm pressure. This means water sprays with enough force to clean dishes well. When the filter gets clogged, water flow decreases. This makes cleaning less effective. A filter also helps keep the wash water clear. This prevents streaks and spots on glassware. It gives you the sparkling results you expect.

## Exact Location of the Filter in a Kenmore Elite

Finding the filter in your Kenmore Elite dishwasher is straightforward. You do not need to pull the dishwasher out. You also do not need special tools. The filter sits in an accessible place. It is designed for easy removal and cleaning.

When I first looked for my Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter, I thought it would be hidden. But I found it quickly. It sits right in the middle of the bottom. This makes it simple to access. Knowing its spot helps you keep it clean.

### Where to Look First

Open your Kenmore Elite dishwasher door. Pull out the lower dish rack. Look at the very bottom of the tub. You will see a round component. This component is usually made of plastic. It sits in the center of the base. This is where your filter assembly is located.

The assembly often has a handle or a small post sticking up. This is for easy gripping. Some models might have a flat screen covering the entire bottom. The filter part is still a twist-and-pull section within that. It is always near the spray arm.

### Identifying Filter Components

The filter assembly in most Kenmore Elite models has two distinct parts. First, there is the cylindrical fine micro-filter. This is the part you usually twist to remove. It looks like a mesh cylinder. This filter traps small food particles.

Second, there is the coarse filter or filter plate. This is a flat, perforated screen. It sits under the fine micro-filter. It catches larger food debris. You often need to remove the fine filter first to access the coarse filter. Both parts need regular cleaning.

## Step-by-Step Guide: Removing the Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

Removing the filter from your Kenmore Elite dishwasher is a quick process. It does not take long. Following these steps ensures you remove it correctly. It also prevents any damage to the filter or the dishwasher.

I remember my first time removing the filter. I was a bit nervous. But once I tried it, I saw how easy it was. Now, it is a routine task for me. It takes less than a minute.

### Safety First

Before you begin, make sure the dishwasher is off. You do not need to unplug it. Just ensure no wash cycle is running. This prevents accidental starts. It also keeps you safe from moving parts or hot water.

Always let the dishwasher cool down if it just finished a cycle. The water inside can be hot. Wait a few minutes for the temperature to drop. This simple safety step protects you during the process.

### Twisting and Lifting

Open the dishwasher door. Pull out the lower dish rack. You might need to remove some dishes if they are blocking access. Locate the cylindrical filter at the bottom center. It usually has an arrow or an unlock symbol.

Twist the filter counter-clockwise. You will feel it unlock. Lift the cylindrical filter straight up and out. Below it, you will see a flat, coarse filter. You can usually lift this plate out directly. Some models might require a small twist to release it too.

## Cleaning Your Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

Cleaning the filter is the most important part of this process. A clean filter ensures your dishes come out spotless. It also prevents odors from building up inside the dishwasher. This simple step makes a big difference in performance.

When I started cleaning my filter regularly, I saw a huge improvement. My dishes were sparkling. There were no more food specks. It made me realize how vital this small part is.

### Rinse and Scrub

Take the removed filter parts to your sink. Rinse them thoroughly under running warm water. Use a soft brush, like an old toothbrush, to scrub the mesh and surfaces. Pay attention to any trapped food particles. Ensure you remove all visible debris.

For greasy residue, use a small amount of mild dish soap. Scrub gently with the brush. Rinse again until the water runs clear. Make sure no soap residue remains. You can also clean the flat coarse filter. Rinse it well and scrub any stuck-on bits.

### Dealing with Stubborn Debris

Sometimes, food particles can get really stuck in the mesh. Do not use sharp objects to poke them out. This can damage the filter screen. Instead, try soaking the filter parts in warm, soapy water for 10-15 minutes. This helps loosen stubborn grime.

After soaking, scrub again with your soft brush. For very tough stains or mineral deposits, a mixture of equal parts white vinegar and water can help. Let the filter soak in this solution for a bit. ## Reinstalling the Filter Properly

After cleaning, reinstalling the filter correctly is essential. A poorly installed filter will not work. It can also cause problems with your dishwasher's performance. The steps are simple and mirror the removal process.

I always double-check the installation. I want to make sure it is secure. This ensures the dishwasher runs smoothly. It prevents any leaks or poor wash results.

### Aligning and Locking

First, place the flat coarse filter back into its position. Make sure it sits flat at the bottom of the tub. There are usually small grooves or tabs to guide it. This ensures it fits perfectly.

Next, take the cylindrical fine micro-filter. Line up the tabs on its base with the slots in the coarse filter or the dishwasher tub. Gently push it down. Then, twist it clockwise until it locks into place. You should feel a click or a clear stopping point. Some models might have an arrow that aligns with a "lock" symbol.

### Final Checks

Once the filter is reinstalled, give it a gentle tug. Make sure it is secure and does not wobble. If it feels loose, repeat the alignment and twisting steps. A secure filter prevents food particles from bypassing the system. It ensures clean water circulates.

Push the lower dish rack back into place. Close the dishwasher door. You are now ready to run your next load of dishes. You should notice a significant improvement in cleaning performance. This simple act of maintenance makes a real difference.

## Signs of a Clogged Kenmore Elite Dishwasher Filter

Your Kenmore Elite dishwasher will give you clear signals if its filter is clogged. These signs tell you it is time for maintenance. Ignoring them can lead to bigger problems. Pay attention to how your dishes look and smell.

I learned to recognize these signs quickly. It helps me act fast. This prevents more significant issues with my dishwasher. Knowing what to look for saves time and hassle.

### Poor Washing Performance

The most common sign is dishes that are not clean. You might see food particles stuck to plates or glasses. There might be a gritty film on items. Sometimes, you will notice white streaks or cloudy glassware. These are all signs that the wash water is not clean enough.

A clogged filter restricts water flow to the spray arms. This reduces the water pressure needed for effective cleaning. The dirty water then recirculates, redepositing grime onto your supposedly clean dishes. This defeats the purpose of running the dishwasher.

### Unpleasant Odors

Another clear sign of a clogged filter is a bad smell. Your dishwasher might smell like rotten food or mildew. This odor becomes stronger each time you open the door. Food particles caught in the filter decompose over time. This creates a foul smell.

The smell can transfer to your dishes. Even after a wash cycle, dishes might carry a faint unpleasant odor. Regular filter cleaning eliminates this issue. A clean filter means a fresh-smelling dishwasher and fresh dishes. A dirty filter can also lead to issues like mold. ### Water Drainage Issues

In some cases, a severely clogged filter can impact drainage. You might notice standing water at the bottom of the dishwasher tub after a cycle. The filter sits near the drain pump. If it is blocked with food, it can hinder water from reaching the pump.

This issue can also be a sign of a blocked drain hose or pump. However, checking the filter is always the first step. A simple filter clean can often resolve drainage problems. ## FAQ Section

### Q1: How often should I clean my Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter?
You should clean your Kenmore Elite dishwasher filter at least once a month. If you frequently wash heavily soiled dishes, or have a large family, cleaning it every two weeks is better. This schedule prevents food buildup. It also ensures consistent cleaning performance.

### Q2: Can I run my Kenmore Elite dishwasher without the filter?
No, you should never run your Kenmore Elite dishwasher without the filter. The filter protects the pump and prevents large food particles from recirculating. Running it without the filter can damage internal components. It will also leave your dishes dirty.

### Q3: What happens if I do not clean the filter?
If you do not clean the filter, food particles will accumulate. This leads to poor washing performance, leaving dishes with food residue or a gritty film. It also causes foul odors inside the dishwasher. A severely clogged filter can even affect drainage.

### Q4: Do all Kenmore Elite dishwashers have a removable filter?
Most Kenmore Elite dishwashers manufactured in recent years feature a removable, manual filter system. This design is common in quieter, energy-efficient models. Some older or more basic models might have a hard food disposer that does not require manual filter cleaning.

### Q5: How do I know if my filter is broken?
Inspect your filter for cracks, tears in the mesh, or missing pieces. If the filter parts do not lock securely into place, they might be broken. A damaged filter cannot effectively trap debris. This will lead to dirty dishes or pump problems. A broken filter usually needs replacement.
