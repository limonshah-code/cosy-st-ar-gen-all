## Maintaining Your Shark Vacuum for Optimal Performance

Proper maintenance ensures your Shark vacuum continues to perform well. Regular care extends the life of your machine. It also keeps your home clean and free of pet hair. I always make sure to keep up with these simple steps.

First, **empty the dust cup regularly**. For pet owners, pet hair and dander fill up the dust cup quickly. Emptying it after each use prevents clogs and maintains strong suction. Many Shark vacuums have an easy-to-empty design. This makes the process quick and mess-free.

Second, **clean the filters**. Most Shark vacuums have foam and felt filters. Some also have a post-motor HEPA filter. These filters trap dust and allergens. Rinse the foam and felt filters with water once a month. Allow them to air dry completely before putting them back. Check your HEPA filter as per the manual. This keeps your vacuum's filtration system working properly.

Third, **check the brushrolls**. Even with Zero-M technology, some hair or debris might wrap around the brushroll. Periodically inspect the brushrolls for tangled hair or obstructions. Remove any blockages carefully. This maintains efficient cleaning performance. A clean brushroll ensures good contact with your hard floors.

Fourth, **inspect hoses and attachments for blockages**. Pet hair can sometimes get stuck in hoses or nozzles. If you notice a drop in suction, check these areas. Clear any blockages using a long, slender object. Avoid using sharp objects that could damage the hose.

Following these simple maintenance steps keeps your Shark vacuum in top condition. This ensures it remains the best tool for tackling pet hair and keeping your hard floors spotless. Regular care means consistent cleaning power.
